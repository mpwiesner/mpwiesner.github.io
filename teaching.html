Teaching Experience



Guest lecture on October 23, 2015 in ASTR 363 at Purdue on the rings of the Jovian planets. Available as a PowerPoint file or a PDF file.

Guest lecture on September 21, 2015 in ASTR 263 at Purdue on light and electromagnetic radiation. Available as a PowerPoint file or a PDF file.

Guest lecture on August 28, 2015 in ASTR 363 at Purdue on time, the celestial sphere and the motion of the Sun. Available as a PowerPoint file or a PDF file.

I have taught physics and astronomy in various capacities since 2002.

My first experience was as a Teaching Assistant in physics labs at Marquette University. During the 2005-2006 year, I taught 5 labs one semester and 4 the next semester.

In Fall 2006 I completed student teaching for licensure in physics at Rufus King International School in Milwaukee. My current teacher licensure is in Illinois and is for physics (grades 9-12) and general science (grades 6-12). My licensure information can be found at the Illinois State Board of Education.

I taught physics for incoming 9th graders in the Upward Bound program at Marquette University in Summer 2006 and Summer 2007.

I taught both calculus-based and trigonometry-based physics at Carroll University in Spring 2007. I also taught introductory astronomy at Carroll in Fall 2007 and Spring 2008.

I taught Introductory Physics at Marquette University in Summer 2007 and Summer 2008.

I taught conceptual physics and freshman biology full time at the Milwaukee Academy of Science for the 2007-2008 school year. I also founded the high school choir club at MAS.

At Northern Illinois University, I taught introductory astronomy from 2009-2014. I had class sizes well over 100 students some semesters.

At Northern Illinois University I received several awards for my teaching, including the university Outstanding Graduate Teaching Assistant Award for 2013.

I also ran the NIU Observatory, leading many tours of the sky and teaching thousands of people about our amazing universe.

Right now I teach Saturday Morning Astrophysics at Purdue and do other outreach events.
