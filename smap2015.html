Saturday Morning Astrophysics Spring 2015



Saturday Morning Astrophysics takes place at Purdue University in the Physics Building Room 234.

SESSION 1: THE SOLAR SYSTEM

In Session 1, we discussed the structure of the solar system and the properties of the planets.

The slides for this talk are here: The Solar System

We also did an activity to calculate the mass of the Sun from the distance between the Earth and the Sun and the length of the year: Kepler's Third Law Activity

Websites for Further Information
Astronomy Picture of the Day
Stellarium (Night Sky Simulation Software)

SESSION 2: STARS

In Session 2, we introduced how stars are formed and what they are made of. We watched a section of "The Life and Death of a Star" from "The Universe" series.

We also discussed how astronomers learn about stars by breaking light from stars up into a spectrum. We demonstrated how this is done using spectrum glasses and a glowing pickle. The slides for this talk are here: Introduction to Stars

Finally students were challenged to classify stars by type by using spectral templates.

The templates are here: Star Matching Templates and the matching challenge is here: Star Matching Challenge

SESSION 3: GALAXIES

In this session on March 14 (Pi Day!) we gave an introduction to the main types and properties of galaxies and completed an activity to find the age of the universe by using Hubble's Law.

The calculator for this session is here: Hubble's Law Calculator 1.

We also had a cake to celebrate the birthday of Albert Einstein, born on this day in 1879.


SESSION 4: GRAVITATIONAL LENSING

In this session, on April 11, Angie van Sistine introduced gravitational lensing, the bending of the path of light by massive objects. The talk she gave is here: Lecture_on_Lensing.

We then introduced students to Galaxy Zoo, the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) and the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) Archive , classifying galaxies and looking at examples of gravitational lensing in the SDSS and HST archives. The activity sheet for this session is available here: Activity_4_Worksheet .

SESSION 5: INTRODUCTION TO THE NIGHT SKY: SESSION I

On May 9 we gathered at the Jefferson High School Planetarium for an introduction to the summer night sky. Mr. Bill Huston used the planetarium to show us planets we can see right now as well as to point out how to recognize summer constellations.

SESSION 6: INTRODUCTION TO THE NIGHT SKY: SESSION II

In our final session of the 2014-2015 school year, we met at the West Lafayette Observatory at Cumberland Elementary at 9:30pm on June 20 (June 13 was too cloudy) to observe the night sky. We practiced finding summer constellations and planets and we looked at a few objects in the Observatory's telescope, including especially the planets Jupiter and Venus and the crescent Moon. We learned how to use star charts like this one: Monthly Sky Maps
