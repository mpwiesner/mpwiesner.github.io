Saturday Morning Astrophysics



Saturday Morning Astrophysics takes place at Purdue University in the Physics Building Room 234.

Here is the description of our activities in Spring 2015.

You can register here for the 2015-2016 season of Saturday Morning Astrophysics.

These are the sessions we will have this year:

SESSION 1: Telescopes (September 11, 2015)

We opened the year of SMAP by presenting All About Telescopes, an introduction to the physics of telescopes. We then built Galileoscopes and learned to use them. Instructions for how to build a Galileoscope are located here: Galileoscope Instructions.

SESSION 2: Cosmic Rays (October 10, 2015)

In our second session Dr. David Sederberg led an investigation of cosmic rays using cloud chambers. We then learned more about cosmic rays by reading a brief handout on cosmic rays. Students were challenged to answer a set of questions on cosmic rays and finally and we watched short video on cosmic rays.

SESSION 3: Exploding Stars (November 14, 2015)

In this session Dr. Rodolfo Barniol-Duran gave an introduction to supernovae (exploding stars). We also watched a brief video about energy so that we would be prepared for the activity. We then completed an experiment simulating exploding stars using bouncing balls, exploding Mentos and Diet Coke and flying ping-pong balls. You can also see some photos from this lesson on the Purdue College of Science Facebook page.

SESSION 4: Meteorites and Cratering (December 12, 2015)

In this session, Dr. Matt Wiesner gave an introduction to meteorites and cratering (also here as a PowerPoint file). We also watched a movie about the formation of craters on the Moon, a movie about the effect of a giant asteroid impact on the Earth and we looked at the website for Impact Earth, a site that simulates impacts with different parameters. We then completed an activity simulating the formation of impact craters by dropping rocks into sand, flour, water and soap. Sample videos can be viewed here:
