Students in WiesnerLab at Benedictine University



Christian Estrada (major in physics) graduated from BenU in 2018. Christian worked with me on a search for merging galaxies in the Sloan Digital Sky Survey Stripe 82 coadd.

Malik Ata (major in health science) graduated from BenU in 2019. Malik also worked on the search for merging galaxies. Malik is now in medical school.

Stephen Tomsky (major in physics) graduated from BenU in 2020. Stephen worked on the merging galaxies project and also worked on the effort to include lensed host galaxies in the LSST-DESC DC2 project.

Zohia Arif (major in biology) graduated from BenU in 2020. Zohia worked on analysis of spectroscopy of white dwarfs, including participating in a night of observing using the Remote Operations Center at Fermilab to operate the SOAR telescope in Chile. Zohia went on to complete the Masters in Integrative Physiology degree at Benedictine.

Ahmad Hamed (major in health science) graduated from BenU in 2020. Ahmad worked on a project listing and analyzing all known strong gravitational lenses. Ahmad presented his work at the 2020 summer meeting of the American Astronomical Society. Ahmad is now in medical school.

Vanessa Hadweh Smith (major in health science) graduated from BenU in 2021. Vanessa looked at a relationship between mass to light ratio and morphology for galaxies in the Hyper Suprime Cam 2nd data release.

Marcelo Avila (major in physics) graduate from BenU in 2022. He began working with me on the Kilonova Data Challenge in Spring 2020. The Kilonova Data Challenge was an effort to simulate colliding neutron stars (kilonovae) in the LSST-DESC DC2 data. Marcelo worked at Fermilab with me in Summer 2021 as part of the Visiting Faculty Program.
